4 Years at NNHS - About Us

Spreading Programming Passion

We're a bunch of students with the common interest of computer programming, 3D modeling, or computer science in general. Instead of programming locked up in our basements with the curtains drawn and lights off, we get together at NNHS to collaborate on projects and further understanding. Most importantly, we want to give NNHS students the opportunity to pursue their CS interests. We have members who've been programming for years, and others for a few days. No matter where you are, we'll have a spot for you.

Interested in games? Web? Apps? Or just need the answers to some school curriculum CS class? We'll hook you up.
Want to help but aren't interested in straight coding? No problem, we also do 3D modeling, game design, hardware, and memeing. You just need to show up with the interest.
Thursday X block RM 430, weekly. Stop by anytime, we give out free programming club t-shirts.


Right now we are looking for a future officer experienced in web dev. We're not the best at making websites and don't really like it, so if you have a skillset in this field please contact us!

Join

We welcome everyone to join us. We don't care whether you have no experience programming at all. We keep close documentation and tutorials to heighten your understanding of the languages we support. Join in and collaborate with us!

Languages

Currently, our club officially teaches and does projects in Python, HTML, CSS, C#, and Lua. We have members who've done all kinds of languages. If you are interested in a language that we don't really do, feel free to take it to us with any project ideas.

Released Projects (2016-2019)

Our projects that are launched. We may still push updates on certain ones depending on their nature.

Tiger Insider Website

We're not that good at making websites from (mostly) scratch, but we scraped this together. It was an interesting learning experience. The Tiger Insider website uses the bootstrap CSS framework and silverstripe PHP for the backend. We fundraise and pay for the hosting ourselves. We also made some graphic art to slap on the website and hired a gfx artist online to make an article placeholder banner. We like to use our own code, so bootstrap CSS was the optimal way in efficiency and creativity. Silverstripe was pretty cool too, but they have really bare documentation and we had many keyboard-smashing moments and server crashes. This website is still in active development.

4+ months development time, since April 2017. (CONCLUDED)

A 3D online game in OOP Lua

We won't mention the name of this game, partly to protect our anonymity if any of our players find out about this website. It's an action combat multiplayer fighting game. We used an object-oriented version of Lua and blender. The physics engine used was roblox studio. We took trigonometry we learned in class and applied it to the game's 3D space. The game is now released on the roblox platform. Over the course of the 9 month development period we hired various gfx artists and 3D modelers online to create art assets. We made a lot of money off this and have lots of players, so we're still updating it.

12+ months development time, since Nov 2016. (STILL CONTINUED)
Played 100,000+ Times Since Release
1,600$+ USD in microtransaction sales (pre-tax)

Orbit - A Space Game

This is one of our more known projects by NNHS students. It's a neat scroller app game to play when class gets boring. We used swift/xcode and even made the music with garage band. It's now released on the app store.

3+ months in development time
Downloaded 10,000 Times Since Release

Dice Simulation

This was a fun mini project made by a member who joined with zero experience. It's in python and simply simulates the outcomes of two rolls of dice. It then has some conditions to see whether the user has won based on some rules.

2-3 meetings time spent, 2016
